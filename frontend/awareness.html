Awareness on Cleanliness and E-waste management

1.7 million tons of e-waste was generated in India in 2021.

What is E-Waste?

  • E-waste includes old phones, computers, batteries, cables, bulbs, and TVs.
  • Contains hazardous substances (lead, mercury, cadmium) that pollute water and soil when dumped or burnt in open areas.
  • E-waste is the fastest-growing solid waste stream in India and worldwide.

How Can You Dispose E-Waste Responsibly?

  • Never throw e-waste into regular dustbins!
  • Take e-waste to official recycling centers or collection events (search local listings).

  • Ask electronics stores about old device return/buyback.
Did you know? Only about 10% of e-waste collected in India is formally recycled. The rest ends up harming the environment and health of informal workers.
TIP: Donate working electronics like computers, tablets, and phones. Someone else can reuse what you don't need!

A single AA battery can pollute 60,000 liters of water if thrown in landfill!

The Importance of Cleanliness

  • Reduces diseases: Clean surroundings prevent the spread of dengue, cholera, typhoid and more.
  • Builds pride: Clean streets, parks, and rivers improve quality of life for everyone.
  • Protects urban wildlife and reduces plastic entering lakes and oceans.

How Can You Participate?

  • Throw waste in covered bins. Segregate wet, dry, and hazardous waste.
  • Report dirty spots, overflowing bins or open dumping with CleanZone.
  • Encourage your RWA, school, or office to organize monthly cleanup drives!
  • Say NO to single-use plastics wherever possible.
Did you know? Over 450,000 tons of garbage are dumped on India's land every day. Every bit you collect or report makes a difference.

Citizen Action!

  • Participate in city or community awareness events.
  • Teach family and children to dispose and segregate e-waste properly.
  • Suggest e-waste bins in your area to your local administration or Resident Welfare Association.
  • Use digital platforms like CleanZone to alert authorities to cleanliness issues.
